Phishing. What is phishing and why is it so dangerous for society?

During the last few years, Internet has provided countless opportunities and made things that we thought that were impossible a few years ago. But nevertheless, there is also a bad side of the Internet that is not talked that much and has the potential to be one of the biggest threats to society. One of its most powerful weapons is called "phishing"

First of all, what is phishing?

Phishing is a kind of cyberattack where the cybercriminal tries to steal the victim's money, identity or personal information (such as credit card numbers or passwords) in a website that looks like the original website in which that information is put (like cloning a bank webpage and there making them feel like they are putting their credit card number in a reliable website, but they aren't).

History and first known cases and its evolution through the years.

It is thought that the first phishing attacks happened during the mid-1990s, when a group of hackers that were employees of AOL used instant messaging apps and email to get user's credentials and enter into their accounts.

During the 2000s, phishing attacks became more organized and targeted. The first attempted attack to a payment system is thought to have occurred in june 2001. Between 2004 and 2005, aproximatedly 1.2 million users in the US were victims of phishing. With an aproximate loss of 900 million USD.

Conlussion and thoughts about phishing

To conclude, I think that phishing is one of the most powerful weapons criminals have right now in order to get what they want, being able to even ask for a prce or something in exchange to return them what is of them.

So, I advice to always be careful and make sure you know where are you putting your credentials before putting them and stay safe!
